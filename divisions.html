DIVISIONS WITHIN THE NATIONAL ARCHIVES OF MALAYSIA

DIVISION 1

PLANNING AND COORDINATION DIVISION.

OBJECTIVES :

1. To guarantee that the National Archives' policies, directions, and plans are sound and efficient.

2. To make sure that, in accordance with the officer in question, officers and staff are competent in managing archives and records.

3. Ensuring collaboration and synergy established between the provision of products, effective service delivery, and value addition, as well as ensuring that officers and personnel in public office are aware of and knowledgeable in the field of records management.

4. To guarantee that service delivery systems and records management are implemented properly and in accordance with the rules, regulations, and requirements established by the National Archives.

DIVISION 2

GOVERNMENT RECORDS MANAGEMENT DIVISION.

OBJECTIVES :

1. To make sure that public documents are handled methodically and successfully in accordance with the guidelines and requirements to be kept in good condition and made available to the public.

2. To make sure the Agency Records Center created by the government office adheres to the guidelines established by the National Archives.

3. Ensuring that the electronic records management system used in public offices complies with the National Archives' criteria.

4. To ensure that the destruction of government records is done in a methodical manner and in accordance with the rules established by the National Archives in order to reduce the need for space, cost, and equipment in government offices.

5. To guarantee appropriate management, maintenance, and implementation of the government records disaster plan in public offices as well as the safe management and maintenance of essential government records.

DIVISION 3

ARCHIVES MANAGEMENT DIVISON.

OBJECTIVES :

1. Establishing an archive collection that includes audio-visual and personal archives.

2. Registering for the acquisition of archive items in both the Archival Management System (AMS) and the Computer Archival System (COMPASS)

3. Constantly preserving digital records using AMS while simultaneously applying specialised storage management for audio-visual materials to preserve audio-visual archive resources.

4. Providing referral and access services for audio-visual archive materials as well as digital archives materials that are kept in AMS, as well as preserving physical archive items using a variety of restoration methods and procedures.

DIVISION 4

MANAGEMENT SERVICES DIVISION.

AMENDMENTS IS BEING MADE BY THE :

1. Development and Maintenance Section.

2. Administration and Human Resources Section.

3. Financial and Asset Management Section.

4. Records Management and Registry Section.

5. Information Technology Section.

DIVISION 5

DEVELOPMENT DIVISION.

OBJECTIVES :

1. Maintaining archives in accordance with established national heritage standards.

2. Providing access to archival material in order to encourage its use as a source of reference and research.

3. Using archival information to increase historical awareness and appreciation.

4. Extending the reach and application of archival content via new technologies, exhibitions, archives, and study.

5. Increasing the number of people who visit the National Archives and learning about the planned events and programmes.

DIVISION 6

RESEARCH MANAGEMENT AND DOCUMENTATION DIVISION.

OBJECTIVES :

1. To make sure that relevant parties have access to records and information regarding boundaries, sovereign rights, present concerns, and national historical truths.

2. Organising and oversee the department's printing and publication plans as well as take care of the publications' sales and distribution.

3. Conducting study on historical occurrences, locations, and individuals by gathering relevant data and resources for public consumption.

DIVISION 7

STATESMEN ARCHIVES DIVISION.

OBJECTIVES :

1. To make sure that the nation recognises and values the role and contributions of leaders and statesmen.

2. To guarantee that the artefacts and collection from the statesmen are maintained as proof of their leadership and accomplishments.

3. Establishing the Statesmen Archive as a hub for historical teaching and study.
